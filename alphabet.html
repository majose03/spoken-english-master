Important Phonics Rules

1. Silent Letters

Many English words have letters that are written but never spoken. Examples: Knee (nee), Write (rite), Lamb (lam).

2. The "TH" Sound

TH has two sounds: a hard voiced sound (This, That, Then) and a soft voiceless sound (Think, Thank, Bath).

3. Soft vs Hard C

When C is followed by E, I, or Y, it sounds like 'S' (City, Center). Otherwise, it sounds like 'K' (Cat, Cup, Cold).

4. Magic E (Silent E)

An 'E' at the end of a word makes the inner vowel say its name instead of its sound. Example: mad → made, bit → bite.
